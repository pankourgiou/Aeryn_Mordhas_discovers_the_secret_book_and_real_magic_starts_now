Elythra
The Ashen Reaches · Domain of Eternal Dusk
Cartographic Survey · Imperial School, Year 412
Geographical & Climatological Overview

Elythra occupies the cold interior basin of the northern continent, enclosed on three sides by the Vorthane Peaks whose rain-shadow creates the characteristic arid highland steppe. The land receives scant precipitation — a mere 180–240mm annually — concentrated in brief but violent spring thaws as snowmelt descends from the peaks. Summers are brief and searingly hot; winters last seven months and regularly plunge below −35°C.

The phenomenon known as the Grey Veil — a persistent inversion layer of volcanic aerosols from the dormant Morthis caldera — suppresses solar radiation and creates the perpetual dim light for which the realm is named. Dust storms arise without warning each autumn, lasting two to fifteen days and reducing visibility to nothing.

Notable Climatic Epochs

Records indicate three distinct climatic phases over the millennium: the Cold Dominion (Years 1–290), an abrupt Warm Interlude (Years 291–540) likely triggered by increased volcanic output, and the Return to Frost (Years 541–present) coinciding with reduced solar activity measured by the Velundran astronomers.

Myrivale
The Tawny Shores · Land Where Rivers Meet the Sea
From the Western Maritime Survey · Year 658
Geographical & Climatological Overview

Myrivale spans a vast lowland coastal plain backed by the Coppertooth Mountains, where three major rivers — the Aurel, the Vayne, and the Thornwick — converge before emptying into the warm Inner Ocean. The climate is a complex semi-arid Mediterranean hybrid: hot, fire-prone summers, mild rainy winters, and a remarkably persistent ochre haze from airborne iron-oxide dust blown in from the eastern plateaux.

The realm's defining meteorological character is its extreme variability. The Myrivale Paradox, documented by climatographers since Year 220, describes how the same territory can experience both drought and flood within a single season — coastal zones receiving seasonal ocean storms while inland valleys remain completely parched.

Flood & Fire Record

The millennium history shows 14 catastrophic flood events (waters exceeding 9m above normal river height) and 22 Conflagration Years — summers so dry and hot that wildfire consumed more than 30% of the forested highlands. Years 701–750 stand as the most volatile half-century, recording both a Great Flood (Year 712) and a Great Fire (Year 731) within two decades.

Sorynthia
The Verdant Isle · Crown of the Inner Sea
Island Survey — Compiled from Seventeen Coastal Expeditions
Geographical & Climatological Overview

Sorynthia is the largest island landmass in the known world, its mountainous spine rising to the snow-crowned Aetherpeaks in the north and descending through dense temperate rainforest to the warm shallow bays of the south. Surrounded entirely by the Inner Thalassian Sea, it enjoys what climatographers call a Sovereign Climate — largely self-regulating, with precipitation recycled through the island's own orographic lifting and dense forest transpiration.

Annual rainfall averages 1,840mm, making it the wettest of the Five Realms. The island's interior hosts permanent glaciers despite its temperate latitude, a phenomenon attributed to the Aetherpeak elevation (4,200m). Cyclonic storms track northward from the tropical zones and make landfall on Sorynthia's southern coast with remarkable regularity — on average 4.7 significant storms per year over the thousand-year record.

The Great Green Record

Sorynthia's forests function as a vast climatological archive. Ring analyses of the Thousand-Year Silverwood specimens have provided the most complete precipitation record of any realm, confirming three major drought episodes (Years 180–195, 440–458, 771–783) and two extraordinary Deluge Decades of record rainfall. The island's biodiversity has never suffered an extinction event directly attributable to climate.

Thaloryn
The Golden Straits · Crossroads of Wind and Tide
The Straits Survey · Jointly Commissioned, Year 780
Geographical & Climatological Overview

Thaloryn is defined by its extraordinary geography: a narrow strait-land between two great seas, where the climate is shaped as much by ocean currents as by sun and latitude. The Thaloryn Corridor acts as a natural wind funnel — prevailing westerlies accelerate to dangerous speeds as they compress through the narrowing, creating the legendary Strait Gales that have sunk more ships than any other weather phenomenon in the known world.

The interior is paradoxically arid despite being flanked by open water. Precipitation shadows created by the flanking Thornback Ridges leave the central valley a near-desert, while the coastal strips receive adequate rainfall. The golden-tan landscape — immortalised in countless paintings — results from iron-rich soils exposed by geological uplift and bleached by relentless wind erosion.

Wind Records

The Anemometric Tower of Valdrath, operational since Year 280, holds records of 47 gales exceeding 180km/h. The absolute maximum — 241km/h recorded in Year 617 during the Three-Day Howl — remains unmatched in any realm. Average annual wind speed at the Strait's narrowest point is 68km/h, limiting agriculture and requiring all construction to be low-profile and heavily anchored.

Eryndor
The Deep Forest Crown · Where River Meets the Mountain's Knee
The Forest Survey · Cartographer's Guild, Year 501
Geographical & Climatological Overview

Eryndor occupies a broad continental interior basin, dominated by the Deepwood — the largest contiguous temperate forest in the known world, stretching over 2.4 million square kilometres. A great river system drains the snowmelt of the northern Ironspire Range southward through the forest, creating an intricate lacework of lakes, bogs, and oxbow wetlands that moderates temperatures and maintains perpetual humidity.

Climate is continental humid: winters are cold and prolonged (avg −12°C in January), summers warm and occasionally hot (avg +24°C in July), with reliable precipitation year-round averaging 780mm. The forest itself acts as a powerful climatological regulator, preventing extremes seen elsewhere in the realms. However, this same moisture creates the conditions for Eryndor's defining meteorological hazard: convective megastorms — vast rotating thunderstorm complexes that can persist for 72 hours and produce devastating tornadoes.

The Tornado Annals

Eryndor's Whirl Scribes have maintained unbroken records of tornado events since Year 88. The thousand-year record documents 2,847 tornadoes of all intensities, including 14 events categorised as Worldenders (path width exceeding 3km, duration more than 90 minutes). The most destructive — the Black Spiral of Year 588 — carved a 340km path through the heart of the Deepwood.
